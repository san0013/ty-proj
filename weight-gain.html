Weight Gain

Gaining weight in a healthy way involves a combination of strength training and a calorie-dense diet. Here are some tips and exercises to help you achieve your weight gain goals.

Dietary Tips

  • Increase Caloric Intake: Consume more calories than you burn.
  • Focus on Nutrient-Dense Foods: Include whole grains, nuts, seeds, dairy, and lean proteins.
  • Eat More Frequently: Have 5-6 smaller meals throughout the day instead of 2-3 large meals.
  • Incorporate Healthy Fats: Avocados, olive oil, and nut butters can add calories without filling you up too much.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink smoothies or shakes instead of just water to increase calorie intake.

Recommended Exercises

  • Squats: Great for building leg and core strength.
  • Deadlifts: Effective for overall muscle gain, especially in the back and legs.
  • Bench Press: Targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps.
  • Overhead Press: Builds shoulder and upper body strength.
  • Pull-Ups: Excellent for developing back and arm muscles.
  • Leg Press: Focuses on the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes.
  • Barbell Rows: Strengthens the back and biceps.
  • Food Options for Weight Gain
  • Breakfast: Paneer Paratha: Whole wheat paratha stuffed with paneer, served with yogurt. Banana Smoothie: Made with milk, banana, and a scoop of protein powder. Poha with Peanuts: Flattened rice cooked with spices and peanuts for added calories.
  • Lunch: A rich rice dish with meat or vegetables, cooked with spices. Chole Bhature: Spicy chickpeas served with deep-fried bread. Dal Makhani with Butter Naan: Creamy lentils served with butter naan.
  • Snacks: Nuts and Dried Fruits Mix: A calorie-dense snack option. Protein Bars: Homemade or store-bought bars with nuts and seeds. Cheese Toast: Whole grain bread topped with cheese and grilled.
  • Dinner: Butter Chicken with Rice: A rich, creamy chicken dish served with white rice. Paneer Tikka Masala: Grilled paneer in a spicy gravy, served with naan. Khichdi with Ghee: A comforting dish made with rice and lentils, topped with ghee.
